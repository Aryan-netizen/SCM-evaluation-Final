About the Thar Desert

The Thar Desert, also known as the Great Indian Desert, spans over 200,000 square kilometers in northwestern India. This golden wilderness forms a natural border with Pakistan and is home to vibrant cultures, ancient forts, and breathtaking landscapes.

Despite its arid conditions, the Thar supports a diverse ecosystem and is inhabited by various communities that have adapted to the harsh environment over centuries.
