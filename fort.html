The Jaisalmer Fort, also known as Sonar Quila, is one of the largest forts in the world.

Built in 1156 AD by Rawal Jaisal, its yellow sandstone walls shimmer like gold under the sun, blending seamlessly with the desert landscape. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of the rare "living forts," with a vibrant community residing within its ancient walls. Its intricate architecture, narrow alleys, and stunning temples make it a timeless symbol of Rajasthan's rich heritage.
